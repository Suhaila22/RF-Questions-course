The gap this closes

Two different problems, one root cause

RF misbehaves in ways ordinary electronics never does — and when a link drops, most teams are left guessing. This course replaces that guesswork with a shared method, from the physics on the bench to the fault on the tower.

The field engineer's problem

A link fades in the rain and recovers by morning. The radios test fine. Without a method, every fault is a guess and every guess is a truck roll.

The electrical engineer's problem

Every wire is a transmission line, every component has parasitics, and a design that looks right on paper underperforms in the real world. RF needs its own foundations.
